Challenge

#HowGreatIsOurGod

Platform DevelopmentExperience DesignSupport
Show Case Study

Overview

Passion is an annual conference that draws thousands of college students together for the Kingdom of God. Year after year, the Passion team pushes production and technology to help communicate to a large audience in a more excellent way. In January 2012, 42,000 college students gathered at the Georgia Dome in Atlanta, Georgia.

Directive

Passion commissioned Whiteboard to build an interactive platform to manage a web stage for the live stream and creative an immersive online experience for viewers.

Solution

We used the Bitgravity platform for streaming and media security. We also utilized a highly customized version of Wordpress to allow the Passion Livestream team to change the availability states of the streamed or recorded videos.

The site itself was an immersive experience offering a live feed pulling from Twitter with over 20 key terms, as well as a contact form for technical problems and a live messaging system to push messages from the tech team to the users during their streaming process. We utilized Amazon EC2 clusters to handle the server load, which at times reached 15 thousand concurrent streaming connections. The Whiteboard team was on-site for much of the conference to field technical problems and ensure stability and the smooth operation of the platform during the event. During the gathering a beautiful moment transpired when #HowGreatIsOurGod became the #1 worldwide trending topic on Twitter.

Real-Time Needs Waiting To Be Met

Prototyping
Show Case Study

Overview

On April 27th, a massive storm outbreak hit the southeast United States producing numerous tornadoes and damage. In the middle of this crisis, Whiteboard built a prototype devoted to needs awareness and response coordination called Castaneed.

Directive

With so much buzzing around the use of social media, we believe that tools like Facebook and Twitter can be leveraged to create viral momentum surrounding real-time needs. We have been asked multiple times if our team believes this prototype could be built to handle this type of collaboration on every continent in the world.

Although our concentration is devoted to creating a successful program in the United States, it would be amazing to see a prototype of this nature being implemented worldwide. Castaneed is devoted to meeting needs and when needs are met the impact could be infinite.

Solution

Research and interviews produced case studies that told our team that in almost every instance of disaster the local church and non-profits in the area are always the first responders to supply need.

Alongside designer and fontographer, Robbie de Villiers and his vision for the identity of the platform- we built Castaneed with a model that would leverage the immediacy of social media, local churches, and non-profits to give people the hands-on task of serving or donating to a specific need. The interface features a map of the United States that will gather serving opportunities and real needs in pursuit of being fulfilled.
